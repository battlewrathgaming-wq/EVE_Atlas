Status: resting context
Date: 2026-06-03
Project: AURA Atlas
Milestone: Atlas Storage And Runtime Hardening

## Purpose

Capture the Human/Overseer landing after HS224 acceptance and HS226 runway opening.

This is not a Dev runway, not doctrine expansion, and not authorization for implementation. It is a recovery note for how Atlas should continue shaping hardening work.

## Stable Landing

Atlas should keep spreading through its local structure like roots until it meets a boundary.

When a boundary is met, Atlas should:

1. name the boundary
2. keep the local side coherent
3. prove the boundary surface with a small fixture/offline or read-only packet
4. decide whether the boundary becomes a real passage

This fits the current storage/runtime method:

- operational usefulness over architectural finality
- one hardening seam at a time
- local proof before real mutation
- fixture/offline authority before operator data movement
- no pipeline-wide proof before the neighboring pieces exist

## Current Application

The current SDE line is:

- topology import/rewrite authority proof accepted in HS224/HS225
- inventory import/rewrite authority proof opened in HS226
- real operator SDE import/rewrite remains parked
- provider-backed SDE download/build remains parked
- combined topology + inventory behavior remains parked

## Testing Principle

Some tests are premature until the neighboring pipeline surface exists.

Early local proofs should show that each seam behaves honestly. End-to-end or pipeline proofs should wait until enough real surfaces touch each other to make the result meaningful.

This avoids false confidence, awkward scaffolding, and accidental pressure to overbuild.

## Residual Watchpoint

After HS226 lands, Atlas should not rush directly into real SDE import/download. A small acceptance review or advisory pause may be useful before opening any real operator mutation or provider-backed download/build packet.
